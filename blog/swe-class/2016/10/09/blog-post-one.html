Blog Post One: An Introduction

Oct 9, 2016

Getting Acquainted

In this first post, I wanted to introduce myself as a software engineer, and the project on which my team and I will be reporting on for our Software Engineering class for this Fall 2016 Semester. I am a Junior at Drew University majoring in Computer Science and Economics with a deep interest in data analytics. I interned this past summer in Boston with Fidelity Investments’ Quantitative Technology group, and the summer prior to that with Corning Incorporated’s Information Security group. Now, in my Software Engineering class, I will be working with a team of students to leverage our collective knowledge and experience to build a real-world software application for a client from Drew University.

Our Client

Our client is a sociology professor at Drew who has spearheaded efforts to provide a directory of services and activities to those new and old in the community of Orange, New Jersey. From arts and festivals, to child services and clinics, our client has collected a vast amount of information from her community in an effort to promote local businesses, and aid newcomers in finding what they need when they need it.

Our Challenge

Our client has enlisted our services to build a rich mobile application that can display the wealth of information in her existing database of business and service providers in Orange to a wide audience. The challenge is not only to create a full-featured, multi-platform application that can deliver this information, but also a low-tech component that intermediaries can use to ‘export’ addresses, contacts, and other information to emails, texts, or other media for users without a smartphone. We are also pushing for a multi-lingual interface as this would dramatically increase the user-base and usefulness of the application in its intended context.

The Framework

We chose Ionic Framework in part because it was the framework of choice for our original mapping protocol, Geotagger. While we may be using a different mapping protocol, the Ionic framework is a perfect choice for our use-case as we are able to build a single application that can be deployed on the web, and both major mobile platforms: iOS and Android.

Organization

So far, we have managed to split our workload up according to the three major components of the application resulting in a View Team, a Model Team, and a Controller Team, each responsible for engineering that particular component in conjunction with the remaining two teams. The three teams are all present at customer meetings with a spokesperson or business analyst of sorts from each so that their questions and concerns are addressed in customer meetings and amongst team members

Forward Outlook

Now that we have gathered the main requirements from our customer, the View Team of which I am a part hopes to create a mock-interface for the customer to demo and provide feedback. We also hope to begin integrating some of the controller logic into the application depending on the progress of that team.

Thanks for reading the first post, and be sure to come back for more over the coming weeks!
